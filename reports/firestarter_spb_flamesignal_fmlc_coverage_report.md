## Root Cause Diagnosis

### 1. Warmup Period Requirements
The formula for `fmlc` in `scripts/visualization/build_top100_evidence_viewer.py` is defined as:
```python
fmlc_na = df_merged['fundingRate'].isna() | df_merged['change_24h'].isna() | high_200.isna() | low_20.isna()
df_merged.loc[fmlc_na, 'fmlc'] = np.nan
```
Here, `high_200 = df_merged['high'].rolling(200).max()` requires **200 historical periods** to calculate a valid value.
Consequently, the first **199 indices** of the time series for *every symbol* will always have `fmlc = NaN`.

For standard symbols, the history consists of **722 bars** in 1H mode and **1442 bars** in 30m mode.
- For 1H mode: `722 total bars - 199 warmup bars = 523 potential valid FMLC bars`. Due to the shift/lag in other indicators, we get exactly **522 valid FMLC bars**, which is exactly **72.3%** of the dataset.
- For 30m mode: `1442 total bars - 199 warmup bars = 1243 potential valid FMLC bars`. We get exactly **1242 valid FMLC bars**, which is exactly **86.1%** of the dataset.
Because these rates are less than 90%, **all 143 symbols are classified as PARTIAL under the strict 90% threshold**, even though their coverage is mathematically complete given the warmup constraint.

## Recommendations and Next Actions

1. **No Formula Patches Needed**: The formula and calculations are working exactly as intended. The FMLC NaN values in the early part of the time series are mathematically required by the 200-period rolling maximum of high price (`high_200.rolling(200)`).
2. **Safe to Keep as Is**: If short-history symbols are desired in the viewer, displaying NaN for FMLC in their early bars is the correct behavior. Attempting to fix this by reducing the warmup window would change the mathematical definition of FMLC.
